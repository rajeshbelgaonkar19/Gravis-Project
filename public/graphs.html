    A bar graph visually represents data using rectangular bars with lengths proportional to the values they represent.

    A pie chart displays data as a circular graph, with slices representing proportions of a whole, making it easy to visualize percentages or proportions at a glance.

    A scatter plot visualizes the relationship between two variables by plotting points on a graph, helping to identify correlations or patterns in the data.

    A bubble chart visualizes three dimensions of data by using bubbles to represent points on a graph

    A line graph displays data points connected by lines, illustrating trends or changes over time, making it effective for showing continuous data trends.
